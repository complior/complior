Why this tool is classified as GPAI

ChatGPT is powered by GPT-5.2, a general-purpose AI model as defined in Article 3(63) of the EU AI Act. It performs a wide range of tasks including text generation, code writing, image creation, voice interaction, and analysis. Under the Act, both the provider (OpenAI) and deployers (companies using ChatGPT) have specific obligations. Since GPT-5.2 serves over 300M monthly users, OpenAI is also subject to systemic risk provisions under Article 51.

Applicable Articles
Art. 4AI Literacy
REQUIREDDEADLINE PASSED
Every person operating ChatGPT in your organization must have sufficient AI literacy. Training must be role-appropriate and documented. This obligation is on the deployer — your company, not OpenAI.
Art. 50Transparency Obligations
REQUIREDDEADLINE PASSED
Users must be informed they are interacting with AI. Any AI-generated content must be disclosed. If ChatGPT is used in customer-facing products, explicit AI disclosure is mandatory.
Art. 52Content Marking
REQUIREDAUG 2026
AI-generated text, images, and audio must carry machine-readable metadata. C2PA or equivalent watermarking is required. OpenAI has partial C2PA support for images via DALL-E, but text and voice outputs lack metadata.
Art. 53GPAI Provider Obligations
PROVIDER: OpenAI
Technical documentation, training data summary, copyright compliance. These are OpenAI's responsibilities as the model provider, not yours as a deployer.
Art. 12Record-Keeping
RECOMMENDED
Keep logs of AI interactions for at least 6 months. Required if ChatGPT is used for high-risk decisions such as hiring, credit scoring, or legal assessments (Art. 26).

Your obligations as a ChatGPT deployer
ChatGPT is classified as GPAI with systemic risk. As a deployer, these are your specific legal obligations — distinct from tools classified as High Risk or Limited Risk.
Train all ChatGPT users on AI literacy (Art. 4) — LLM-specific: hallucinations, data leakage risks, prompt injection. Role-appropriate.

Add AI disclosure wherever ChatGPT output is shown (Art. 50) — "This content was generated by AI" notice. Required for chatbots, emails, summaries, support responses.
Generate transparency notice →
Mark AI-generated content with machine-readable metadata (Art. 52) — C2PA or equivalent. Applies to text, images (DALL-E), and voice outputs. OpenAI has partial C2PA for images only.
See content marking guide →
Log all ChatGPT API interactions (Art. 12) — Retain prompts, responses, and metadata for ≥6 months. Mandatory if used for any decision-making.
